Features






High-performance

Detailed cellular simulations are computationally demanding. The code was built with efficiency and performance in mind. HemoCell has excellent scaling from laptops to over 300,000 cores [1,2,3].

Validated

Both single cell mechanical behaviour and blood rheology were validated with experiments [4], and evaluated through uncertainty quantification [5].

Extensible

The code is designed to be easily extensible. Adding new material models and cell types, and interactions is simple [6].

Advanced features

HemoCell has several advanced features such as internal cell structure, continuous cell inflow boundary, Lees-Edwards boundary, Load-Balancing. [7,8]


Documentation and downloads


The source code of HemoCell is available under the AGPL v3 license (for details see the file LICENSE in the package). HemoCell can be installed on Linux, Mac OS X (Homebrew) and Windows (WLS).

HemoCell 2D


A validated two dimensional lattice Boltzmann model for blood plasma with a discrete element method for both red blood cells and platelets coupled by the immersed boundary method. Key properties: Fortran, Parallelized with MPI, Suspension rheology validation, Easily used to test large sets of parameters

Virtual Human Project

Simulation data produced by HemoCell was used in the creation of the Virtual Physiological Human IMAX movie. It is an educatory movie targetting the general public and aims to show the current state of the art models building up the Digital Twin. The movie is available here on YouTube.

  • Project: UvA
  • Date: 2017
  • Category: Scientific visualization

Novel material model and validations

The material model of the cells and the fluid structure interaction between the cells have been validated thoroughly. The standard experiments for the comparison are optical tweezer stretching, consant-shear Wheeler test, size of cell-free layer, apparent viscosity in glass tubes. For details on the validation and the material model please see [Cellular Level In-silico Modeling of Blood Rheology with An Improved Material Model for Red Blood Cells, Hemocell: a high-performance microscopic cellular library].

  • Project: UvA
  • Date: 2017
  • Category: Model development

Diabetic blood flows

The transport and rheology of chemically hardened red blood cells are compared to experimental data. HemoCell predicts a disruption in margination efficiency as the ratio of diabetic cells grow. [The influence of red blood cell deformability on hematocrit profiles and platelet margination].

  • Project: UvA
  • Date: 2020
  • Category: Rheology

Effects of internal cell viscosity differences

To represent the higher inner cytoplasmic viscosity of deformable cells an fast algorythm is implemented to identify fluid nodes inside the cell and assign different viscosity to them. Due to the fine-tuned algorithms the application of this feature imposes about 4-5% performance penalty only [Numerical Investigation of the Effects of Red Blood Cell Cytoplasmic Viscosity Contrasts on Single Cell and Bulk Transport Behaviour].

  • Project: UvA
  • Date: 2018
  • Category: Model development

Effects of vessel curvature on platelet adhesion

Microfluidic experiments are often done in straight channels and ragular geometries. Here the effects of curvature are investigated, which influence the adhasion of platelets significantly via the appearence of strong elongational flows. [The influence of red blood cell deformability on hematocrit profiles and platelet margination].

  • Project: UvA
  • Date: 2021
  • Category: Microfluidics

Sensitivity analysis and uncertainty quantification

The mechanical model undervent detailed sensitivity analysis, and inverse uncertainty quantification [Inverse Uncertainty Quantification of a Cell Model Using a Gaussian Process Metamodel].

  • Project: UvA
  • Date: 2020
  • Category: Model development

Malaria infected cells

Model for stage V gametocyte cells. The shape has been reconstructud basen on micro-CT images, the mechanics is set based on micro-pipette membrane stretching. [Understanding Malaria induced red blood cell deformation using data-driven Lattice Boltzmann Simulations].

  • Project: UvA
  • Date: 2017
  • Category: Biomedicine

Optimised high-performance code

HemoCell is built using C++ with a clean API and heavily optimised algorithms. It scales well from a single notebook (capable of a handful cells) up to the largest supercomuters (millions of cells). The code was deployed up to 330,000 CPUs.The framework provides effective automatic parallelisation as well as dynamic load-balancing using (preliminary support). Optimizing parallel performance of the cell based blood flow simulation software HemoCell.

  • Project: UvA
  • Date: 2017
  • Category: HPC

Detailed cellular trafficking in blood flows

The simulations give access to the detailed motion and deformation of cells inside blood flow. These trajectories were used to compliment experimental measurements to understand the shear-induced diffusivity and fundamental steps of margination [Red blood cell and platelet diffusivity and margination in the presence of cross-stream gradients in blood flows].

  • Project: UvA
  • Date: 2019
  • Category: Rheology

Pulsatile blood flow in micro aneurysms in the human retina

In case of diabetic diseases, retinal micro-aneurysms often appear as well. These pathologic vessel sections are not fully understood, and can pose a dangerous threat to clear vision. In this project a patient specific micro-aneurysm was reconstructed in 3D using two types of imaging modalities. The trafficking of the cells and the interaction with the vessel wall changes substantially from the healthy cases. For more details please see the related publication [The effect of stiffened diabetic red blood cells on wall shear stress in a reconstructed 3D microaneurysm].

  • Project: UvA
  • Date: 2020
  • Category: Biomedicine

Load-balancing large-scale simulations

Load imbalance is the situation where the workload between processes is unequal. In case of cellular suspension simulations it might arise due to the inhomogeneity of the cell distribution within the simulation domain. This is typically a major source of loss of parallel efficiency for bio-fluid flows simulations such as blood flow simulations. [Load balancing of parallel cell-based blood flow simulations]

  • Project: UvA
  • Date: 2017
  • Category: HPC

Multi-Scale blood flow model

HemoCell is used as a micro-scale modell componet of a large multi-scale model. The aim is to reduce the computational cost while still acquire accurate haematocrit values on the macro-scale. [A Heterogeneous Multi-scale Model for Blood Flow].

  • Project: UvA
  • Date: 2020
  • Category: HPC

Flow conditions necessary for high shear platelet aggrwegation

The conditions at the site of a platelet aggregate initiation were investigated. The aim was to uncover the properties of the necassary flow conditions in order for a high-shear rate platelet aggregation to forms. [Haemodynamic flow conditions at the initiation of high-shear platelet aggregation: a combined in vitro and cellular in silico study]

  • Project: UvA
  • Date: 2019
  • Category: Rheology

The role of the cell free layer in platelet aggregation

The cell free layer provides ample space for the initial stages of the high-shear platelet aggregation. This study compliments experimental findings with detailed cellulara simulations. [Identifying the start of a platelet aggregate by the shear rate and the cell-depleted layer]

  • Project: UvA
  • Date: 2019
  • Category: Rheology
